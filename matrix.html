Standards Reference Key

MA EDIN Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs)

  • PLO 1: Promote, inspire, and cultivate creativity and innovative thinking for learning, service, and leadership.
  • PLO 2: Practice instructional methods and technologies for transformative learning, interdisciplinary problem-solving, and planet-sustainable solutions.
  • PLO 3: Develop competencies as design team leaders and learning facilitators.
  • PLO 4: Engage in lifelong learning for continuous improvement in professional practice and collaborative leadership.

ISTE Educator Standards (2017)

  • Standard 1 – Learner: Educators continually improve their practice by learning from and with others.
  • Standard 2 – Leader: Educators seek out opportunities for leadership to support student empowerment.
  • Standard 3 – Citizen: Educators inspire students to positively contribute to and responsibly participate in the digital world.
  • Standard 4 – Collaborator: Educators dedicate time to collaborate with colleagues and students to improve practice.
  • Standard 5 – Designer: Educators design authentic, learner-driven activities and environments that recognize and accommodate learner variability.
  • Standard 6 – Facilitator: Educators facilitate learning with technology to support student achievement.
  • Standard 7 – Analyst: Educators understand and use data to drive instruction and support students.

OECD Learning Compass 2030

  • Student Agency & Co-Agency: Learners take an active role in deciding what and how they learn, working with teachers and peers toward shared goals.
  • Knowledge: Disciplinary, interdisciplinary, epistemic, and procedural knowledge that enables understanding and action.
  • Skills: Cognitive, metacognitive, social, emotional, and practical skills for navigating complexity.
  • Attitudes & Values: Personal, local, societal, and global values including equity, sustainability, and respect for diversity.
  • Transformative Competencies: Creating new value, reconciling tensions and dilemmas, and taking responsibility.
  • Anticipation-Action-Reflection (AAR) Cycle: A continuous improvement process enabling adaptive, forward-looking practice.

UNESCO Teacher Competency Standards

  • Knowledge & Understanding: Subject-matter expertise, curriculum knowledge, and understanding of educational theories and research.
  • Pedagogical Skills: Instructional planning, classroom management, assessment design, and technology integration.
  • Professional Disposition & Values: Commitment to equity, lifelong learning, ethical practice, cultural sensitivity, and collaboration.
