Artifact 2: Concept Map — Creativity Frameworks in Educational Contexts

Rationale

This artifact is an academic concept map developed as part of a research project exploring the intersection of creativity theories and their practical applications in educational settings. The concept map synthesizes major theoretical frameworks—including Csikszentmihalyi's Systems Model of Creativity, Sternberg's Investment Theory, Amabile's Componential Model, and Torrance's creative thinking processes—and visually maps their connections to classroom-based instructional strategies that foster creative thinking among learners.

This artifact was selected for the portfolio because it demonstrates PLO 1 by showing the ability to identify, analyze, and connect diverse scholarly perspectives on creativity to actionable teaching practices. The concept map serves as both a personal learning tool and a resource for fellow educators who wish to ground their creative pedagogy in established research. By visually representing the relationships between theory and practice, this artifact illustrates the kind of interdisciplinary, innovative thinking that the MA EDIN program cultivates. The process of creating this concept map required deep engagement with scholarly literature, critical analysis of competing frameworks, and the creative synthesis of complex ideas into an accessible visual format.
