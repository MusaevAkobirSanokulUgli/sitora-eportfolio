Artifact 2: Interactive Digital Job Aid for Differentiated Instruction

Rationale

This artifact is an interactive digital job aid designed to support educators in implementing differentiated instruction strategies across diverse classroom settings. Created during the MA EDIN program, this tool provides a practical, step-by-step guide for adapting content, process, product, and learning environment to meet the varied readiness levels, interests, and learning profiles of students (Tomlinson, 2017). The job aid was developed using interactive digital design principles, incorporating clickable decision trees, embedded video demonstrations, downloadable templates, and self-assessment checklists that allow educators to apply differentiated strategies in real time.

This artifact exemplifies PLO 2 by demonstrating the practice of instructional methods and technologies for transformative learning. The job aid addresses a persistent challenge in education: the gap between what educators know about differentiation and their ability to implement it consistently in the classroom (Tomlinson & Imbeau, 2010). By creating a technology-based tool that bridges this theory-to-practice gap, this artifact illustrates how innovative instructional design can empower educators to meet the diverse needs of all learners. The job aid also reflects an interdisciplinary approach by incorporating insights from universal design for learning (UDL), cognitive science, and educational technology, demonstrating the kind of cross-disciplinary problem-solving that PLO 2 emphasizes.

Purpose of This Job Aid

A step-by-step interactive guide designed to help K–12 educators implement differentiated instruction strategies in real time. The tool provides decision trees, templates, and quick-reference checklists for adapting content, process, product, and learning environment.

Quick-Reference Implementation Checklist
Before the Lesson

☑ Pre-assess student readiness levels

☑ Identify learning profiles (visual, auditory, kinesthetic)

☑ Prepare tiered materials (3 levels minimum)

☑ Set up flexible grouping arrangements

☑ Create choice board or learning menu

During the Lesson

☑ Use formative checks every 10–15 minutes

☑ Rotate between groups for targeted support

☑ Provide extension tasks for early finishers

☑ Offer multiple modes of engagement

☑ Monitor & adjust pacing in real time

Universal Design for Learning (UDL) Alignment

Action & Expression Multiple means of demonstrating knowledge
Design Note: This job aid was created using Canva and Google Sites for interactive navigation. Educators can access it on any device and use the embedded links to jump directly to relevant strategies based on their classroom context. The tool incorporates Tomlinson's (2017) differentiation framework and CAST's (2018) UDL Guidelines.
