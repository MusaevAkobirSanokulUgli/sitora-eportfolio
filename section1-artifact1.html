Artifact 1: Design Thinking Lesson Plan for Creative Problem-Solving

Rationale

This artifact is a comprehensive lesson plan developed during the EDIN program that applies the Stanford d.school's Design Thinking framework to a K-12 classroom setting. The lesson was designed to guide students through the five stages of design thinking—Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, and Test—as they addressed a real community challenge identified through stakeholder interviews. This artifact was selected because it exemplifies PLO 1 by demonstrating how structured creative processes can be embedded into instructional practice to foster innovation, empathy, and collaborative problem-solving among learners.

The lesson plan integrates multiple pedagogical strategies, including collaborative brainstorming, rapid prototyping with low-cost materials, and peer feedback cycles. It also incorporates technology tools such as digital mind-mapping software and multimedia presentation platforms, enabling students to express their creative ideas through diverse modalities. The design reflects a deliberate effort to move beyond traditional instructional approaches and create a learning experience that cultivates both divergent and convergent thinking skills.
